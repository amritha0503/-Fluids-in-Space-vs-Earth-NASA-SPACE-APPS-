Understanding Fluid Behavior

üåä Surface Tension Effects

On Earth: Gravity pulls fluid down, limiting surface tension effects to small scales (droplets, meniscus).

In Space: Without gravity, surface tension dominates, creating perfect spheres and enabling unique phenomena like water bridges.

üå°Ô∏è Temperature Impact

On Earth: Heat causes convection currents as warm fluid rises and cool fluid sinks.

In Space: No convection! Heat transfer happens only through conduction and radiation, making thermal management critical.

üì¶ Container Interactions

On Earth: Fluids settle at the bottom of containers due to gravity.

In Space: Fluids stick to container walls and can float freely, requiring special designs for fluid management.

ü•Ñ Viscosity Role

On Earth: Viscosity resists flow, but gravity still pulls fluid downward.

In Space: Viscosity becomes more important as there's no gravity to overcome it, making mixing and separation different.
